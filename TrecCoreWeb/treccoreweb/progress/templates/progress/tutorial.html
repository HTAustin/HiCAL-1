    Tutorial

    Please read the tutorial carefully. If you have any questions, please email one of the study coordinators.

    1. Intro

    For this study, you are required to find and label documents using our specially designed review tools.

    You will be asked to use a judging interface to view document summaries or the full document content and judge the document's relevance to a given topic.

    You are also allowed to enter their own queries and search for documents that they feel will help you find more relevant documents in some tasks.

    The study will involve about six hours of work. The first hour will be a tutorial and the next five hours will be dedicated to spending one hour per search task.

    You are required to complete five search tasks. You should finish the five tasks on your own computer within one week.

    1. In the beginning of the study, you are required to complete a demographic questionnaire.

    2. Next, you will complete 5 tasks. Each task consists of:

    • a pre-task questionnaire,
    • a judging task(Section 3),
    • and a post-task questionnaire.

    3. After completing the 5 tasks, there will be an End-of-Study questionnaire.

    3. Judging criteria

    3.1 Definition of Relevance

    We use three point relevance scales for relevance judgment. The three scales are Highly Relevant, Relevant and Not Relevant respectively.

    Assume that you have the information need stated in the topic and that you are at home searching the web for appropriate material.

    • If the document contains information that you would find helpful in meeting your information need, mark it Relevant.
    • If the document directly addresses the core issue of the topic, mark it Highly Relevant.
    • Otherwise, mark it Not Relevant.

    3.2 Consistency of Judgments

    Be consistent in your judgments. Referring to the topic text from time to time will help you maintain consistency in your judgments.

    The number of relevant or irrelevant documents has no bearing on the "value" or "goodness" of the topic. So, don't feel that you have to "stretch" your definition of relevant.

    Judge duplicate documents the same. In other words, if you judge a document as relevant and find an identical document, judge that document as relevant too.

    3.3 Pitfalls

    Relying too heavily on keywords to make your relevance judgments could be misleading and cause your judgments to be inaccurate. The keyword search feature is provided as an aid to help you identify the areas of the document that may relate specifically to your topic. Keywords are not meant to be relied upon exclusively as a means to make the relevance judgments .

    Don’t work too fast or too low. You should try to work as fast as possible while maintaining your accuracy.

    3.4 How to judge

    While viewing a document you can judge it in two ways, using keyboard shortcuts or using your mouse.

    3.4.a. Keyboard shortcuts

    To speed up the process of judging documents, you can use the following keyboard shortcuts to judge documents.

    H: marks the document as "highly relevant"

    R: marks the document as "relevant"

    S: marks the document as "not relevant"

    Clicking on one of those keys while viewing document will mark the judgment of the document.

    3.4.b. Mouse clicks

    Beside the document content, you will see three buttons similar to the buttons below. Please click on the button to judge the document relevancy.

    4. Proper Behavior of User Study

    This scientific research study requires your full attention. If you are unable to give this research your full attention, please excuse yourself from the study. In particular, while working on a task:

    • You should not use your mobile phones during the study [Please keep your phones on silent mode]
    • You should not listen to music during the study
    • You should not use the computer for checking email, or other unrelated activities during the study
    • We would prefer it if you could do each task from start to end without interruption. If you need to take a break, you should close the application. To resume working, simply start the application back up.
